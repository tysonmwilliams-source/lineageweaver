**Register:** Middle English, Norman‑French, place‑name–derived

**Structure:** Alphabetical by letter → Female names → Male names

**Purpose:** Noble houses, landed families, clerical records, and fantasy worldbuilding.

**Blending Rule:** Per letter and sex, approximately **12% of names** will intentionally draw from *adjacent medieval cultures* (e.g. Celtic-border, Norse-influenced, Occitan, Iberian, Germanic, or Latinate court forms). These are lightly adapted to fit local phonotactics and spelling conventions, creating a natural medieval melting pot rather than a modern multicultural effect.
